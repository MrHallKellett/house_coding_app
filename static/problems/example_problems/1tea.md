Example Round 1 Problem D - Tea (*)

Henry is going to the nearest port to buy tea. Unfortunately, he was caught speeding by naval police, and have fined him for speeding. Henry wants to know how bad the fine was.

Please help Henry find how many less boxes of tea he can buy due to the fine. (Note that Henry can’t buy fractional boxes of tea)

Input Format

The first and only line consists of three integers, m, f, c, the amount of money Henry has initially, the amount of the fine, the cost of one box of tea.

It is guaranteed that Henry can pay the fine.

Output Format

A single integer, the number of boxes of tea Henry couldn’t buy due to the fine.

Example 1

Input:

4 2 3

Output:

1

Explanation:

Henry can originally buy 4 / 3 = 1 box of tea. However, after the fine he only has 4 - 2 = 2 dollars, and thus can’t buy any tea. Therefore, he can buy 1 - 0 = 1 fewer boxes of tea, so the output is 1.

Example 2

Input:

100 21 10

Output

3

Note: When taking input, do not print any message. For example, input() or input(““) would be fine, but input(“Number: “) or input(“Something“) might fail the tests.
